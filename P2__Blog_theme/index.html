The Day I Learned To Point

The day I learned to point was not like any other day. It was a day that one could even point to on a calendar. I would even say it was a Tuesday. Others would say Wednesday. But my memory can never forget that wonderful Friday. As I sat in front of the television watching Saturday morning cartoons, I knew that the weekday was a mere sunset away.

On that day, I had learned to point.

On that day, I Lived.

Hop, Skip and a Splash

As I hopped and skipped and splashed all over the rain soaked ground, something drastic had finally occurred to me. Something that I can never not unsee, let alone let go. My stylish and truly profound hat only had a spot for one of my ears. Why oh why had I never noticed when I purchased it at Ross.

At Ross, you dress for less. A new meaning of the phrase will forever be forever etched into my one ear holed hat.

Belle of the Ball

At the County Ball I pondered on the jovial encounters I happened to partake upon. I pondered the eloquent intricacies that the local folk happen to attire that evening. Left and right, I get asked “Are you lost?”. I simply smile and with glee “No my friend. The Belle of the Ball is never lost”.

Encounter after encounter I smiled and replied with the rehearsed retort. Twas not till the clock stroke twelve that I had gleaned to discover, that the Belle of the Ball was not at the Ball. But was in the graceful embrace of The Ball of E.D.M.
